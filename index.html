CoRe-MoE: Contrastive Residual Mixture of Experts for Multi-Terrain Humanoid Locomotion with Gait Adaptation

Kailun Huang1,2,†,  Zikang Xie1,†,  Yanzhe Xie1,†,  Yanheng Mai1,  Yi Gu1,  Renjing Xu1,*
1 Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou)    2 Guangdong University of Technology
† Equal contribution    * Corresponding author

Abstract

Humans primarily rely on walking and running to adapt to complex terrains, without employing additional complex or unnecessary motions. Humanoid robots should also focus on smooth switching between walking and running. However, simultaneously achieving gait transitions and multi-terrain adaptation in a unified policy is challenging due to gradient conflicts and terrain-induced visual and dynamic variations. Although Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) can mitigate multi-skill interference, direct joint training often fails to produce clear expert specialization, limiting the effectiveness of MoE. To address these challenges, we propose CoRe-MoE, a two-stage reinforcement learning framework integrating adaptive gait policies, residual corrections, and contrastive MoE training. The first stage trains stable walking and running behaviors with smooth transitions, while the second stage introduces a residual MoE branch guided by contrastive learning, generating terrain-aware motion adjustments and encouraging expert specialization across different terrains. Simulation experiments demonstrate superior success rates, locomotion stability, and multi-terrain adaptation compared with baseline approaches. Zero-shot deployment on a Unitree G1 robot further validates the framework, achieving robust walking and running on stairs, slopes, steps, obstacles, and natural outdoor terrains, while maintaining accurate foothold placement and dynamic stability under external disturbances.
